Documentary

Bully (originally titled The Bully Project) is a 2011 documentary film about bullying in U.S. schools. Directed by Lee Hirsch, the film follows the lives of five students who face bullying on a daily basis. Bully premiered at the 2011 Tribeca Film Festival. It was also screened at the Hot Docs Canadian International Documentary Festival and the LA Film Festival. Bully had its global premiere at Italy's Ischia Film Festival on July 17, 2011. Bully was acquired by The Weinstein Company immediately after its premiere at Tribeca Film Festival. The film was released in United States theaters on March 30, 2012. On the official website the filmmakers are promoting Bully as an "important" advocacy against bullying and facilitating an anti-bullying "movement." Contrary to the filmmakers goals, the film has been critiqued for its inaccessibility in theatres, and the extended downtime between theatre and home release. The film is set to be released on Blu-ray and DVD on February 12, 2013, with pre-orders available through Amazon.com.
